About Me

I am currently a PhD student in the Data Sciences and Operations department at the USC Marshall School of Business. I am advised by Vishal Gupta and Paat Rusmevichientong in the Operations Management group. My research is focused on developing data-driven decision-making methods that blend estimation and optimization. I am primarily interested in solving optimization problems in data scarce settings which arise when i) data collection is expensive, ii) signal-to-noise ratios are low, or iii) systems are highly time inhomogeneous, i.e. the amount of relevant data per uncertain parameter in an optimization problem is often very small. I am interested in applications in healthcare, transportation, matching, and recommender systems. Previously, I obtained by M.S. and B.S. in Operations Research at Columbia University.
Interests. Data-Driven Optimization, Machine Learning
