About Me

I am an Assistant Professor of Operations Management at the Zicklin School of Business at Baruch College.

I obtained by PhD in Operations Management at the Data Sciences and Operations department at the USC Marshall School of Business. I was advised by Vishal Gupta and Paat Rusmevichientong in the Operations Management group. Previously, I obtained by M.S. and B.S. in Operations Research at Columbia University.

My research is focused on developing data-driven decision-making methods that utilize decision-aware or end-to-end learning. These methods are essential for solving optimization problems in data scarce settings which arise when:

  • Data collection is expensive
  • Signal-to-noise ratios are low
  • Systems are highly time inhomogeneous.

I am interested in applications in healthcare, transportation, matching, and recommender systems.
Interests. Data-Driven Optimization, Machine Learning
